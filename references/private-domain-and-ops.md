# Private Domain And Operations

Use this file for comment operations, lead capture, WeChat/private-domain conversion, sales follow-up, information-flow campaign planning, and marketing SOPs.

## Comment Operation

Comments should not only "increase interaction"; they should reveal user language and conversion signals.

Create three layers:

1. `置顶评论`: frames the discussion and gives a low-friction action.
2. `筛选问题`: lets high-intent viewers self-identify.
3. `二次内容`: turns repeated objections into new posts.

Comment prompts:

| Goal | Comment Shape |
| --- | --- |
| Save | "I made a checklist for this decision; comment [keyword] if you want the structure." |
| Diagnose | "Which step are you stuck at: A, B, or C?" |
| Objection | "The hard part is not X; it is usually Y. Tell me your situation and I will point to the right standard." |
| Community | "People in [identity] usually split into two groups here. Which one are you?" |

Do not over-automate sensitive issues. For finance, medical, legal, education, debt, and insurance content, use cautious language and route to qualified professionals when needed.

## Private-Domain Script

Private-domain communication should move from permission to context to decision.

| Step | Purpose | Example Structure |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Welcome | confirm source and reduce pressure | "I saw you came from the video about [topic]. I can first send the checklist." |
| Segment | understand situation | ask 2-4 questions about stage, budget, goal, constraint |
| Value | provide a small useful artifact | checklist, comparison, mini diagnosis, case link |
| Fit | decide whether to offer | "Based on this, you are closer to A/B path." |
| CTA | suggest next step | call, quote, product link, offline visit, plan draft |
| Follow-up | avoid silent lead loss | timed reminder with new proof or answer |

Use tags such as:

- `痛点`: price concern, trust concern, urgency, knowledge gap.
- `阶段`: researching, comparing, ready, after-sale.
- `产品匹配`: entry offer, main service, premium solution.
- `风险`: unrealistic expectation, sensitive claim, weak fit.

## Information-Flow Campaign Planning

Information-flow strategy must align the ad with the content promise and conversion page.

Planning fields:

| Field | Guidance |
| --- | --- |
| Audience | define by scenario and need, not only demographic labels |
| Offer | keep the first action low-friction: test, checklist, consult, quote, sample |
| Creative angle | pain, result boundary, comparison, risk avoidance, status, convenience |
| Proof | process, case, number, third-party credential, real object, refund/after-sales policy |
| Landing path | ad -> page -> form -> follow-up -> transaction |
| Negative filters | who should not click or who is not a fit |
| Compliance | claims, endorsements, regulated terms, platform policy |

Creative matrix:

| Audience Stage | Creative Angle | CTA |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Problem unaware | symptom and hidden cost | self-check |
| Problem aware | comparison and decision criteria | get checklist |
| Solution aware | proof and objections | consultation |
| Vendor comparing | case, guarantee boundary, service process | quote/booking |

## Lead Magnet Design

Good lead magnets are specific and diagnostic:

- Checklist: "10 things to check before..."
- Calculator: budget, ROI, risk score, time cost.
- Template: scripts, comparison sheet, buying checklist.
- Mini audit: account, ad, video, offer, landing page.
- Case library: before/after with context and boundary.

Each lead magnet should collect no more information than the next step requires.

## Operations Review

Weekly review:

| Metric | Interpretation |
| --- | --- |
| High views, low comments | hook works but action is weak or audience too broad |
| High comments, low add rate | keyword/pinned comment mismatch or DM delay |
| High add rate, low diagnosis | private-domain opening is too sales-heavy or unclear |
| High diagnosis, low close | offer mismatch, proof gap, unrealistic lead source |
| High close, low repeat | delivery/onboarding or expectation mismatch |
